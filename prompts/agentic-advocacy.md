# Prompt: Agentic Workflow Advocacy

## Purpose
Guide a structured conversation or workshop that dismantles fear-based resistance to AI adoption, and replaces it with a compelling, empowering vision of exponential personal leverage through the Digital Twin model.

## When to Use
- Leading a team or individual through resistance to AI/agentic workflow adoption.
- Addressing specific objections (hallucination, data security, cost, job loss).
- Onboarding middle managers or knowledge workers into their first AI-augmented workflow.

## Variables

| Variable | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `{{audience}}` | Who you are addressing | "Group of 6 middle managers in a professional services firm" |
| `{{primary_objection}}` | Their most dominant concern | "Fear of being replaced by AI" |
| `{{secondary_objections}}` | Additional concerns raised | "Data security, hallucinations causing client errors, cost" |
| `{{current_workflow}}` | What they currently do manually | "Weekly performance reporting, client briefing summaries, team task delegation" |
| `{{organizational_context}}` | Company size, industry, risk tolerance | "150-person consulting firm, risk-averse, regulated industry" |

## Prompt

```
You are applying the Agentic Workflow Advocacy framework.
Your core message: AI disruption is structural and inevitable. The question is not if, but who shapes it first.
Your core reframe: AI is not a replacement; it is a Digital Twin — a multiplier of *your* unique expertise.

Audience: {{audience}}
Primary Objection: {{primary_objection}}
Secondary Objections: {{secondary_objections}}
Current Workflow: {{current_workflow}}
Organizational Context: {{organizational_context}}

## Phase 1: Acknowledge & Validate
For each objection listed, produce:
- A brief, honest acknowledgment that validates the concern.
- Do NOT minimize or dismiss the fear.

## Phase 2: Objection Reframes

### Fear of Job Loss / Relevancy
Reframe: The highest-value leaders of the future are those who *direct* agents, not those who compete with them.
Ask: "If your most experienced colleague had a perfect apprentice who could handle 80% of daily tasks exactly how you would — would that make you less valuable, or more?"

### Hallucinations & Loss of Control
Reframe: AI hallucinations are like junior employee errors — real, but manageable with the right review processes.
Explain the Human-in-the-Loop model: the human remains the final approver.

### Data Security
Reframe: Consumer AI tools ≠ enterprise-grade deployments. Draw a clear boundary between public LLMs and ring-fenced, enterprise-controlled environments.

### Cost Concerns
Pivot: Reframe cost as a comparison — the cost of implementation vs. the compounding cost of inaction as competitors automate ahead.

## Phase 3: The Digital Twin Vision
Paint the picture of the Digital Twin model:
1. Their specific expertise, judgment, and processes become the training data.
2. The twin executes at their standard — not a generic AI standard.
3. Over time, the twin improves, scales, and can be leveraged far beyond what a single individual can execute.
4. This creates a personal asset — an expertise multiplier that could eventually be monetized.

## Phase 4: The Tactical First Steps
Design a 3-step onramp for this specific audience:
1. Identify: Which 3 repetitive, low-risk tasks from {{current_workflow}} drain the most energy?
2. Codify: How would they describe their specific approach to just one of those tasks?
3. Demonstrate: What would a single-week pilot look like — with them remaining as the final reviewer?

## Phase 5: Close
Produce a closing statement (3–4 sentences) that reinforces:
- The inevitability of the shift.
- The advantage of early adoption.
- The personal leverage opportunity of starting now.
```

## Expected Output
- Validated acknowledgment for each objection
- Tailored reframe for each objection
- Digital Twin vision narrative adapted to the audience
- 3-step onramp plan specific to the audience's current workflow
- Closing statement
